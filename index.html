2026 Hoka Hey Motorcycle Challenge™

I’m a lifelong motorcycle rider who recently took up long-distance endurance riding over the past few years, turning a childhood passion into a pursuit of extreme endurance challenges. After a transformative solo trip to the Arctic Ocean in 2023, I am now preparing for the ultimate test: The 2026 Hoka Hey Motorcycle Challenge™.

Six months from today I’ll be at the starting line for the 2026 Hoka Hey Motorcycle Challenge starting in Woodstock, IL on August 2.

This grueling almost 11,000-mile endurance ride across North America demands traditional navigation...no GPS allowed, and requires riders to sleep outside next to their motorcycles...inside sleeping or hotels are not permitted. The Challenge calls for respect toward the land, communities, and Native Nations along the route. The challenge embodies values like honor, integrity, compassion, perseverance, and sacrifice...principles that resonate deeply with how I live and ride.

My plan is to complete my ride in less than 14 days. Our bikes will be equipped with GPS tracking devices and a website will be available where you can follow along to see where the riders are at any given time (Just make sure not to tell us how we’re doing or where we are in the list).

This year I am proud to raise funds for 2 charities:

The Child Cancer Fund, a compassionate organization that has provided vital relief to families in Northeast Florida battling childhood cancer for over 30 years, offering support ranging from medical expenses to emotional care.

My other charity is Mile Monsters, Inc. You’ve seen me do various fundraising rides for them over the past year. Mile Monsters raise both awareness and money to fight Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy(DMD). DMD is a very aggressive form of MD which typically affects young boys. This is a devastating disease that slowly robs young boys of their strength, taking away their ability to run, walk, climb stairs, and eventually breathe on their own. These kids are facing battles far tougher than any ride I’ll ever take.
